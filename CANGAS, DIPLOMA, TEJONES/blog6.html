Gawahon Eco Park


The word “gawahon” itself as a Hiligaynon word means “overlooking.” Yes, it provides an overlooking view of the beauty of nature and creation, all wrapped into a single place.The primary attraction that they have are the seven waterfalls. Yes, you’ve read it right — seven waterfalls located within the area of the Gawahon Eco Park.

There are also frogs, caterpillars, and other animals necessary for the food chain of the butterfly that are bred here. If you wish to learn more about the entire life cycle of a butterfly, there is a viewing room on the 2nd floor where you can watch an educational audio-video documentary of the butterfly.Each waterfall is different and unique from the others. That is why if you get to visit here, you can witness the vibrant and flourishing beauty of seven unique waterfalls! How cool is that right? Come and enjoy this magical place.
